Interest in IT

What is your interest in IT? I’m interested in how IT is constantly evolving. Although the progress of IT back in the day may have been extremely slow nowadays things are upgrading faster than ever and I want to work in an environment that reflects just that. I’d constantly be learning new things and I’d never have a stale day at work. Alongside this I enjoy figuring out how things work which is why I’m very interested in the programming aspect of IT.

When did your interest in IT start? Back when I was in primary school, IT was always my favourite class. Since then I've just always had an interest in it.

Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? I wanted my own laptop since my brother had just started High School and got his own but no one wanted to give me one. Why did a primary school student need one anyway? Even though no one decided to get a laptop eventually my uncle found one of his old work laptops and let me keep it. It was that moment then that REALLY sparked my love for IT. I mean like, I had always liked computer class in primary school but that old laptop was what really sold it for me. It had so many things wrong with it that I was constantly researching and diagnosing it. Turns out I liked how I could learn new things about the physical laptop I was holding and what it was capable of.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT? My brother also went here for his Associate’s Degree and told me that it was the best place to go for anything IT related. I had also attended the open days and really liked what I saw. Alongside this, I was at RMIT last year for my High School classes so I was already a bit familiar with the layout. You've also got those cool rocking chairs on that one floor which has become my new favourite place to work at.

What do you expect to learn during your studies? Lots of different types of IT-related things. Almost like a bit of everything? Of course I’ll major in an area that interests me the most but the Bachelor of Information Technology is, in my opinion, extremely broad and has the first year covering all different aspects of IT. I like this approach though as it allows people to experience everything before having them decide what they want to be an experts in.

Project Ideas - 1

Project Idea: Developing a game using software such as the Unity game engine or RPG Maker.

Overview: An interesting idea I would like to pursue is creating a video game in a software such as Unity or even RPG Maker. If Unity, it would probably be something generic like a third-person game where you fight enemies but I would add my own twist to it through extremely unusual weapons, armor, level design, color-use, sound and enemies. Like, EXTREMELY unusual. There might even be a fantasy-like element to the game through the use of magic. Or maybe only all weapons. If made in RPG Maker however, I’d make it a classic save the prisoner game but have a big plot twist at the end :”) Something like the prisoner isn’t who you thought they were all along or maybe that you were the prisoner all along!

Motivation: I think this project will be interesting because it’s going to be different. Sure, you might have heard that a million times before but I would love to be able to make something REALLY out of the ordinary. I wouldn’t follow the “norm” except for when it comes to fundamentals (press spacebar to jump) and I’d have complete freedom in how I want to approach things. Although people don’t really like change, being able to be completely free with my project will make me proud of it no matter what happens. However, if I were to use RPG Maker, I would take heavy influence from the Legend of Zelda series (specifically the Toon Link series) as they have the “save the princess” formula down pat. I have always been a fan of this series and making a game that could relate to that series would be great. As for the usefulness of my idea, I’m sure someone out there in the world would enjoy my game. Or maybe my game will end with a big life lesson and completely wow all the players as the credits roll?

Description: Regarding my Unity game idea, I’d give my game a really aesthetic name. Everything sounds cooler that way. I’d probably call it “Silent Sweetheart” or “Sumire” which means violet in Japanese. Yes yes very aesthetic. The plot would most likely be based around randomly generated dungeon floors with the last boss after ten or so floors. I’m not sure if there will be a story of it it’ll just be about clearing levels. Combat will follow the fundamentals of most third-person games. This means the game will play as if it was like Dark Souls. Even though lots of games are being made with the same gameplay elements as Dark Souls, the Dark Souls element is relatively new and isn’t too burnt out yet. Because of this, there’ll be a stamina bar as well as a health bar. Weapons won’t be limited to swords. You’d have anything you can see at your disposal. This included picking up items (such as a barrel) to using a whip as a weapon. The art style would probably be low-poly with pastel colours. Although I mentioned dungeon floors earlier, every floor would be different. One floor might be your generic dungeon room but the next might have you outside in an open-world area. The main objective for each room will still be to make it to the end. I think the game might be based around a tower which you climb up with each “floor” of the tower being a new world. Replayability will be a big part of my game with randomly generated dungeons. That way, players would be able to replay over and over and experience at least one thing new every time.

As for my RPG Maker game idea, I’d take a lot of ideas from The Legend of Zelda. Namely how you had to travel to different islands/areas by a literal boat or “Spirit Train”. I remember being very fond of this type of gameplay because not only did each new area or island have heaps to do but your way of arriving at those destinations was only limited by your imagination. The game has lots of opportunities for open-world gameplay, allowing you to fish in the sea or make small stops at small towns with your train. I want to somehow emulate this type of freedom in my own type of game. I believe an airship with the ability to travel to different parts of the country would allow my players to have heaps of freedom in where they want to go. Whether it be to another major city or to a small fishing town on the edge of the sea. I might follow the Legend of Zelda formula of having to go to certain areas to obtain the correct items to assist in beating the last boss or I might make it very open world with RPG-levelling elements and questing The RPG Maker battle system only allows for turn-based combat and 2D gameplay so a lot of the game is experienced through text and visuals. Replayability probably won’t be as big a part in my RPG Maker due to the limitations of text. Reading the same text over and over gets stale extremely fast. I could make up for this with a longer storyline or lots of side quests, etc.

Project Ideas - 2

Tools and Technologies: I would require the use of the Unity game engine, a mediocre gaming computer (we’re talking probably an i3 CPU, 8GB RAM, at least 128GB storage and an average/below-average graphics card. We’re not remaking Crysis here. Just making a fun little game. Although the better the hardware, the more opportunities to work with..) I’d also need the basics like a computer monitor, headset, mouse and keyboard, etc. The software ‘Blender’ would be used for 3D assets and for sound design I would ask my friend (a music producer) for some assistance. Some open-source tools I would consider using is Open Match (only if I wanted to implement multiplayer into my game) and possibly Super Character Controller. Open Match is an open-source matchmaking system created by Google and Unity together and Super Character Controller is a character controller solution that incorporates basic collison and pushback mechanics among other things. Although SCC is no longer updated, it would be a great starting place for character movement.

Drawbacks and Obstacles: A major drawback would be my experience with this sort of project. Being my first ever game, it would probably end up super clunky with lots of bugs and glitches. Alongside this, another drawback would be my storytelling skills. I can’t put anything I think of in my mind to paper so the game may not have a story.. A main obstacle would be time. I would have to learn from scratch how to use Unity and Blender among other things which could end up taking a majority of my project time up. Ways around both of this are limited. Constant testing and debugging could eliminate a lot of the bugs but when it comes to learning how the software works I’d just have to be extremely persistent and believe in my abilities.

Skills Required: I’d probably use a lot of online guides to figure out how Unity and Blender work among other things like graphics-tweaking and basic sound design. The shaders and game itself will have to be hard-coded through Unity also. I don’t think there will be too much trouble with this consider there are heaps of guides online but nothing works perfectly the first time so there’ll be a lot of trial and error involved. External special hardware might include premade assets through the Unity store for things I wouldn’t really be able to create in Blender. Using pre-made assets would be a last resort however because it’s not something created by me. A majority of what I need to complete this project will be learnt through online guides and research.

Outcome: If the project is successful then I will have created my first ever video game! A strange third-person monster fighting game. My original problem of not having knowledge with any of the software will have been solved as I would now know the basics when it comes to making a video game. I’ll forever be knowledgeable in the process of games development and programming.
